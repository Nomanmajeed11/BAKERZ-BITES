Patties

Types Of Patties

1. Beef Patty: A classic choice made from ground beef, seasoned to perfection, and grilled or fried to juicy perfection, often served in burgers or as a standalone dish.

2. Veggie Patty: A plant-based alternative typically made from ingredients like beans, lentils, or vegetables, providing a flavorful and nutritious option for vegetarians and vegans.

3. Chicken Patty: A savory delight crafted from ground chicken meat, seasoned with herbs and spices, offering a leaner alternative to beef patties, commonly used in sandwiches or wraps.

4. Fish Patty: A seafood lover's delight, made from flaked fish such as salmon or cod, mixed with breadcrumbs and seasonings, fried until golden brown, and served with tartar sauce.

5. Lamb Patty: A flavorful choice made from ground lamb meat, seasoned with aromatic spices like cumin and coriander, grilled or pan-fried to perfection, and often served in Mediterranean or Middle Eastern cuisine.

Sendwich

Types Of Sendwich

1. Club Sandwich: Triple-decker delight with layers of turkey, bacon, lettuce, tomato, and mayo.

2. BLT: Classic combo of bacon, lettuce, and tomato between toasted bread slices, with a creamy touch of mayo.

3. Panini: Italian-style grilled sandwich featuring various fillings like cheese, meats, and veggies pressed between crusty bread.

4. Reuben: A deli favorite composed of corned beef, sauerkraut, Swiss cheese, and Russian dressing on rye bread, grilled to perfection.

5. Grilled Cheese: Childhood comfort food made with gooey melted cheese sandwiched between buttery, crispy bread slices, perfect for dipping in tomato soup.

Bread

Type Of Bread

1. Sourdough: Known for its tangy flavor and chewy texture, sourdough bread is made through a fermentation process using naturally occurring lactobacilli and yeast.

2. Baguette: A long, thin loaf with a crisp crust and a soft interior, originating from France. It's typically made with wheat flour, water, yeast, and salt.

3. Rye Bread: Made partially or entirely with rye flour, this bread often has a denser texture and a slightly tangy flavor compared to bread made with wheat flour.

4. Ciabatta: An Italian bread characterized by its irregular shape, porous texture, and chewy interior. It's made with high-gluten flour, water, salt, yeast, and sometimes olive oil.

5. Challah: A traditional Jewish bread enriched with eggs and sweetened with honey or sugar, resulting in a soft, slightly sweet loaf often braided before baking. It's commonly enjoyed on Sabbath and other Jewish holidays.
